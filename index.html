Me

I'm EJGames.
Thanks for reading.

I'm a programmer. I have made many games in the past, and a couple of Python utility apps. And a website (this one you're looking at right now).
I started developing games in early 2020, and, well, let's just say I've learnt a lot since.

My stuff

Locked In

This is a top-down shooter that I'm currently working on. I started it in November(ish) 2021, and it's still going (as of March 2022)! In Locked In, you collect coins, fight enemies, and complete quests (soon to be added).

ConnectCircle (Old)

This is a mobile game I made (surpise: using Unity) where you tap different coloured circles to get points. I even added reward ads to the game, and could get $0.10! But I need to get at least $100 to redeem it.

ConnectMore (Old)

This is another mobile game, but this time, you are dragging around a box to form patterns. This was my first proper mobile game I ever made, and it's available for Android only (because iOS is a pain).

Obby Showdown

This is a roblox game I made! You have a controller, who places stages of parkour for the others to complete. If everyone dies, the controller wins. If the time runs out, the remaining runners win. Wow! I spent August 2021 making this amazing game!

Apolycalypse (Old)

This was an FPS (first-person shooter) game that I made in Summer 2021. Let's face it: it didn't look great. But still, it was 3D, and kind of had a story, but I gave up before finishing the 3rd part of the story.

3D Quiz V2 (Old)

So, I basically got a bunch of pre-made graphics and glued them together with coding. It was a more advanced version of 3D Quiz, which was the first game I made! It had random questions, data saving, and more!

Gun Mayhem! (BETA)

Oh, I tried. Who'd have thought people would prefer washing cars to shooting people? I made a Roblox FPS game, and people would rather ride a cart around a used car battery!? Which was actually quite good.

Epic Gun Battle!

This was the pinnacle of my entire programming career. You know why? Because I didn't write a single line of code. Or, at least I don't think I did. You were basically using Roblox default guns to fight each other.

Practise Basic Obby Skills

I tried to get people to play a game called that? 'Ah, what are you playing? Looks like fun!' 'Yeah, just playing Practise Basic Obby Skills, want to join?' It was a typical Roblox obby game that I branded as a 'practise' place.
