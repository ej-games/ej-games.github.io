The FPS game that never got finished. Not just because of the really bad graphics (it was 3D! Do you expect me to know how to 3D model?), or the gripping narrative (the man tells you to kill zombies). It was just because I had another idea. Which came next:
Obby Showdown (on Roblox)
Ah, you thought I would never branch out and make games outside of Unity? Well you were wrong! My amazing Roblox game got barely any players, and also broke after I left it and came back. Although, the idea behind the game was absolutely amazing. People were completing the obstacle course, while a 'controller' added stages, in real time. Sick. Even after all that effort, I got barely any reward. Just goes to show how hard work doesn't always lead to success. Unless...
ConnectMore
To be honest, it was my first mobile game that
  1. Looked decent
  2. Was 'weirdly addicting'
  3. Had quite a lot of content.
You moved a square, and then it attached to other squares, and you had to build patterns out of the squares. It was a pretty good game. I even added skins, and a pattern viewer, using my newly learnt knowledge of Resources.Load() in Unity. And, also, that was when I finally realised the architecture of Unity games. How did I only just realise what static actually meant?
