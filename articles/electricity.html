Odds & Ends


I heard the saying "volts jolt, and mills kill". And then wondered, why? Then spent about 2 or so hours finding out.

Intro

The saying "volts jolt, and mills kill" (or something along those lines) just means that Current (or Amperage) is the deadly part of an electric shock. Now, that's pretty much the case. But, the voltage does matter as well - just not as much.

I have done extensive research (that just means more than usual) around this topic. Take this with a pinch of salt, though, because most of my sources are quite old. Hopefully, the laws of physics haven't changed much in the past 20 years.
