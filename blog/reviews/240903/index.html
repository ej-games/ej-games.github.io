Welcome to the first sporadic review from Vectarray - reviewing the Nikon Coolpix L340, one of the most affordable bridge cameras on the market. Despite only being first released in 2015, this camera features a 20 megapixel sensor, and 28x optical zoom. A good deal for a camera you can get for less than Â£150 second-hand.

Technical Specs


The Coolpix features:
  • 28x optical zoom
  • 20 MP sensor (5164x3873 at 4:3)
  • Automatic scene detection
  • Continuous autofocus
  • 720p HD video at 30fps
  • ISO sensitivity up to 1600
While this is a great basic bashed-up compact camera, it's got a few features for the intermediate photographers. Just imagine what you can do with customisable exposure settings!

But unfortunately, to cut costs, Nikon decided they wouldn't put a rechargeable battery in this camera. Which means for the rest of your life, you will be swapping out 4 AA batteries in and out of the camera, until you drop dead, probably still with a pack of batteries in your hand.

Image Quality

Yes, the resolution of this camera is acceptable, and 20 MP is plenty for basically every personal use case.
But, the colours in the photos from this camera are sub-par, although it is impossible to take a photo that displays the problem fully. Compare these two photos of spools of wire:

Video Quality

The video quality of the Coolpix is very underwhelming. Colour in the videos is almost non-existent.

Compare that to the colour depth of the photos, and you'll see that filming with the Nikon is practically useless - you'd be better off using your phone. Unless your phone video quality is even worse.

Ease of Use

The Nikon's user experience can only really be described as Standard. All of the buttons are in the same place as most other cameras, there are sufficient ports for most needs, and there are plenty of menu items.

There are only a few problems I have experienced with this great high-quality camera:

Deleting Items

Yes, it's a real pain when you delete a photo that you wanted to keep. Well, it's now a thing of the past, with the Nikon Coolpix L340's ultra-secure deletion system.
Here's how to use it:

Step 1: Open up the photo menu
Step 2: Press the delete key in the bottom right
Step 3: Press "OK" to select "Delete current image"
Step 4: Press the up arrow to navigate to "Yes" when asked, "Are you sure?"
Step 5: Press "OK" to finally delete the image!

You may be thinking, "surely there's a way to delete multiple images!" Well, you'd be absolutely right.
But if you had thought, "surely there's a quick way to delete multiple images!", then you'd be wrong.

Instead of selecting "Delete current images", you can select "Delete selected images". This will open up a menu with a grid of photos. Simple - just navigate through the grid and delete the photos you want. Except it's not "Simple - ", because the grid you're shown isn't actually a grid, it's just a list of photos that has multiple layers. Meaning, you can't go up and down. So the experience of deleting multiple photos is like the experience of navigating through a Word document using only the left and right arrow keys.

Lens Motor

This may just be a problem with my model, but it's still a problem.
The motor controlling the zoom lens on the camera is not only slow, but begging to be set free. When you zoom in, it makes a sort of whimpering sound and takes a fair while to fully zoom to 28x, which is especially annoying when you find a moving object at a distance.

This isn't the only psychological issue with the motor - it also has incredibly low confidence. When you're zooming back out, and you stop, the camera sort of second-guesses itself, and moves the lens back out slightly. Making another slightly sad motor noise.

Good things about the camera

It's cheap. And has good zoom for the price. That's all.

Conclusion

Yes, I know I keep coming back to the price, but the camera is cheap, and has a good number of features. If you want image quality, pick a different camera, but if you want one that just gets the job done, and can capture memories and all that sentimental stuff, then this one's fine.
